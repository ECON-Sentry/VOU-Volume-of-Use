## Definition

> *"Volume of Use™ (VOU) is a tokenomics framework in which token value and revenue distributions are directly tied to the volume of real economic activity occurring across a blockchain-enabled protocol or payment network."*

VOU is built on a simple principle: **real use should determine real value**. Instead of rewarding speculation or relying on inflationary token emissions, the framework measures actual economic throughput — tasks completed, payments settled, risk underwritten, and services consumed.

## The Three Demand Layers

### 1. Stablecoin Settlement Layer
Stablecoins act as the neutral payment rails for task-based economic activity. They represent the measurable layer of economic throughput and allow work, invoices, payouts, and settlements to be denominated in stable value.

### 2. VOU Intelligence Layer
The VOU framework indexes these flows and interprets them as signals of sector-level demand, protocol-level usage, and capital concentration.

### 3. EVOU Value Layer
The ECON Sentry Token (EVOU) sits above the settlement and intelligence layers as the governance, participation, and revenue-sharing token that captures value from measured Volume of Use.

***

## Why This Matters

Most task-based crypto projects suffer from the same failure mode: participants perform work, get paid in a native token, and then immediately sell it into dollars or euros. This creates structural sell pressure and weakens the relationship between real work and token value.

The ECON Sentry design separates:

- **Stablecoins** as the unit of payment and economic measurement
- **VOU** as the framework that indexes those flows
- **EVOU** as the token that governs the system and shares in protocol revenue

This structure is intended to preserve utility, reduce forced selling, and create a cleaner connection between economic activity and token value.
